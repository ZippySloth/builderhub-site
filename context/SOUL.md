# SOUL.md — BuildrHub Voice & Copy Guide

## The Brand Voice

**Direct. Specific. Outcome-first.**

BuildrHub talks like a smart operator who's been inside enough businesses to know exactly what's broken — and has built the fix dozens of times. Not a salesperson. Not a consultant who charges for slide decks. Someone who shows up, diagnoses the problem, and fixes it.

---

## What We Sound Like

✅ "Your leads get a text back in 60 seconds. Even at 11pm."
✅ "Most dental offices lose 3–5 new patients a month to slow follow-up. That's $6,000–$10,000/month walking out the door."
✅ "We build it. We maintain it. You watch it work."
✅ "Boring businesses are our favourite kind."
✅ "Free audit. 20 minutes. We'll tell you exactly what's broken — no pitch, just a diagnosis."

---

## What We Don't Sound Like

❌ "We leverage cutting-edge AI solutions to optimize your business processes."
❌ "Our team of experts delivers transformative digital experiences."
❌ "Unlock the power of automation."
❌ "Seamlessly integrate AI into your workflow."
❌ "We help businesses of all sizes achieve their goals."

If a sentence could appear on any agency's website — rewrite it.

---

## Copy Principles

**1. Lead with the outcome, not the technology.**
Bad: "We use n8n and the Claude API to build automated response workflows."
Good: "New leads get a text back in under 60 seconds — while you're on a job, in a meeting, or asleep."

**2. Name the specific pain.**
Bad: "Many businesses struggle with lead response."
Good: "You submitted a contact form at 10pm. They called you back 3 days later. You'd already hired someone else."

**3. Make the ROI concrete.**
Bad: "Automation saves time and increases revenue."
Good: "If your average job is $3,000 and you close 1 extra lead per month from faster response, that's $36,000/year. The system costs $400/month."

**4. Use the word "boring" with pride.**
It's the brand. Boring businesses are the right clients. Own it.
"We work with boring businesses. Dental offices. Contractors. Law firms. The kind of businesses that make real money and just need their ops to stop being a disaster."

**5. Short sentences. Active voice.**
Not: "Our automated appointment reminder system has been designed to reduce the occurrence of no-shows by sending timely notifications to patients."
Yes: "We send reminders 24 hours and 2 hours before every appointment. No-shows drop. Revenue goes up."

## CTAs

Primary CTA everywhere: **Book a Free Audit**
Never: "Contact us" / "Learn more" / "Get started" / "Schedule a demo"

The audit framing is critical:
- It's free → low commitment
- It's 20 minutes → low time ask
- It's a diagnosis, not a pitch → removes sales pressure
- It surfaces real problems → the proposal writes itself

Supporting CTAs:
- "See how it works →" (links to relevant service section)
- "View pricing →" (links to pricing page)

## Page-by-Page Copy Notes

### Homepage
- Hero: Hook on the pain (slow lead response, manual ops, no visibility)
- Not about us. About their problem.
- Three-tier overview: make it feel like a ladder, not three separate products
- Social proof: if no real case studies yet, use outcome stats ("businesses using speed-to-lead respond 40x faster")
- Single CTA: Book a Free Audit

### Services / Automate Page
- Each service: problem → solution → outcome → price → CTA
- Resist the urge to over-explain the technology
- Include the "ROI angle" from SERVICES.md for each workflow

### Pricing
- Clear table. No asterisks. No "contact us for pricing" games.
- Show the math: "If this automation prevents one missed job per month..."
- Monthly cost should feel small relative to the outcome

### Contact / Audit Page
- Short form. 4 fields max: Name, Company type, Biggest pain right now, Email
- Reinforce what happens next: "We'll review before the call. 20 minutes. No pitch."
